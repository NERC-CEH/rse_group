# UKCEH RSE Group Weeknotes - 21/06/2024

We may not have had weeknotes last week as we were all travelling to Lancaster for the first in-person meeting, and public introduction of, our RSE group.

It was an intense couple of days, half of it dedicated to how we evolve norms for working together (like extending a [team agreement](https://github.com/NERC-CEH/rse_group/blob/main/team/agreement.md) between ourselves), and half of it involving rapid Q&A sessions with different research groups. Soil bioacoustics was definitely a fascinating topic which there'll be a queue to work on.

Jo and Alba spent a day at a hybrid meeting organised by the Turing Institute on "People in Data", developing community and shared skills frameworks - [notes in the discussion here](https://github.com/NERC-CEH/rse_group/discussions/14)

Alba simultaneously gave a well-received talk to a [CEEDS](https://ceeds.ac.uk)(Centre of Excellence in Environmental Science) seminar on building data science pipelines.

We spoke with a couple of different groups with strongly related image machine learning workflows and are testing the water about the extent to which we develop that work in public with an open source licence.

Jo also visited an evening talk for Edinburgh's Women in Data and AI meetup and heard about the BBC's Responsible AI principles - they include some assessment of environmental sustainability of models, so I sent a letter of introduction to the EDS design group and a link to [Kelly's recent keynote](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BSiHHq68In8) at the Collaborations workshop.
